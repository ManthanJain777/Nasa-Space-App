Mission Impact

Enabling Long-Term Sustainability

Zero Waste to Landfill

87% of all mission waste is recovered and reused. Remaining 13% consists of irreducible biological waste safely composted for regolith enrichment.

Energy Independence

Recycling consumes 78% less energy than manufacturing from raw materials. System operates on solar power with battery backup.

Mission Extension

Self-sufficiency in replacement parts and tools enables mission duration extension from 3 to 5+ years without resupply.

Technology Transfer

Innovations developed for Mars recycling are now being adapted for Earth-based circular economy applications.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Unlike Earth-based recycling that often downcycles materials, GALACTIC HORIZON uses advanced plasma reformation and molecular reconstruction to maintain or improve material quality. Our AI-driven sorting achieves 99.2% accuracy, and the closed-loop system operates in Mars' low-pressure environment with minimal energy input.

The 13% of waste that cannot be recycled consists primarily of biological waste and heavily contaminated materials. Biological waste undergoes aerobic composting and is mixed with Martian regolith to create enriched growth medium for greenhouse operations. Contaminated materials are encapsulated and stored for eventual return to Earth or future processing as technology advances.

ARTEMIS-RCL operates on 12-18 kW depending on processing intensity. The system is powered by the habitat's solar array with battery backup. Peak processing occurs during high solar availability. Energy recovery from plasma reformation returns approximately 8-11 kW to the grid, making net consumption just 4-7 kW.
